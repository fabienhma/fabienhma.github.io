Applied Mathematics and Computer Science @ Brown University

Hi! I'm Fabien.

I'm a Hong Kong-born, Taipei- and Shanghai-raised Applied Mathematics and Computer Science student at Brown University, currently finishing up my 3rd year. Prior to starting at Brown, I took a gap year to become fluent in French and to learn the art of French cuisine in Vichy and then Paris.

Before I decided to study Computer Science, I wanted to be an engineer – I wanted to build cool things that would help people. In that regard, I haven't changed much: I'm now interested in software engineering, which consists building cool stuff that helps improve peoples lives.

As an avid powerlifter and fitness junkie, I'm particularly interested in the growing intersection of fitness and technology. As technology improves, we gain more tools to improve our lives. Wearable tech gives us statistics about the way we live and how to improve them. Motion-capture devices are being used by chiropractors to help figure out where muscular imbalances are, and how to fix them. There's an app that helps athletes and coaches alike log and then optimize training, telling you how much fatigue your body has accumulated. There's even an app that teaches you how to do yoga from the comfort of your own bedroom. And that's just the tip of the iceberg!

I am constantly trying to learn more and get better, be it through online courses or mini-projects like this website. I firmly believe in the power of good habits – and I try my best to form them!

In my free time, I am a redditor trying to absorb as much information as possible, a powerlifter fighting with Brown administration to start a club, an aspiring chef trying to make the most out of his meal plan, a volleyball player who is best at setting but is always yearning to hit, a beatboxer constantly in awe of the vocal talent of his a cappella group, Harmonic Motion, and a pretty bad gamer trying to climb the ranks of Overwatch and Hearthstone.

Here's some of the stuff I've been doing in my free time:

Android app

Most recently, I've been learning how to create Android apps via a great udemy course by Rob Percival, The Complete Android N Developer Course . As I've been learning about developing in Android, I've been working on a productivity app, Sessions. It began as a project for me to curb cellphone usage while at the gym. The general premise of it is that you can create "sessions" with set session lengths, break lengths, and notification types for different activities, and upon unlocking your phone, the app will warn you when you've reached your pre-assigned "break" length. It's not yet a done deal – I'm still working on it! But the source code is here , if you'd like to take a look at it. It'll be up on Google Play (hopefully) once I'm done!
